SMORG: Simulator Management System

SMORG is an advanced simulator management system developed for The Pride Centre, Kenya Airways’ training and testing hub. It streamlines the scheduling, allocation, and maintenance of simulators for pilot training across multiple aircraft types including Embraers, Boeing, and Airbus A380. The system supports administrators, instructors, and maintenance engineers, ensuring optimized simulator usage, compliance, and cohesive training operations.

Project Overview & Goals

To design a centralized system that manages flight simulators at The Pride Centre. The goals include efficient scheduling, real-time maintenance tracking, instructor-student coordination, and compliance reporting. SMORG aims to reduce downtime, maximize simulator usage, and provide an intuitive interface for all stakeholders.

Final Design & Solution

  • Scheduling Engine: Automated allocation of simulators to instructors and trainees.
  • Maintenance Tracking: Real-time updates for engineering teams on simulator health.
  • Multi-Aircraft Support: Coverage for Embraer, Boeing, and Airbus A380 simulators.
  • Role-Based Dashboards: Tailored views for admins, instructors, and engineers.
  • Compliance Reporting: Audit-ready logs of simulator usage and training sessions.

Impact & Results

  • Operational Efficiency: 35% reduction in scheduling conflicts.
  • Uptime: Improved simulator availability with proactive maintenance alerts.
  • Training Quality: Streamlined instructor-student coordination.
  • Scalability: Supports future expansion with new aircraft types.
  • Compliance: Enhanced auditability for regulatory bodies.

Key Features

  • Automated simulator scheduling & allocation.
  • Instructor and trainee management.
  • Real-time maintenance alerts for engineers.
  • Compliance and training history logs.
  • Role-based dashboards for admins, instructors, and engineers.

Technologies & Tools

  • Design & Prototyping: Figma, Sketch
  • Collaboration & Planning: Miro, Jira
  • Backend & APIs: Node.js, ASP .NET, Simulator Hardware Integrations
  • Analytics: Power BI, Google Analytics

Challenges & Solutions

  • Challenge: Balancing simulator demand with limited resources.
    Solution: AI-driven scheduling to optimize usage.
  • Challenge: Minimizing downtime due to maintenance.
    Solution: Proactive maintenance alerts and engineer dashboards.
  • Challenge: Supporting multiple aircraft simulators.
    Solution: Flexible architecture for Embraer, Boeing, Airbus A380 and future models.
